I am a Rails Developer currently based out of Portland, Oregon working for New Relic. Originally an aspiring neuroscientist, I now spend my days helping to build the latest and greatest websites using Ruby on Rails. I have been writing software since 2009 and in 2010 founded goodbre.ws, a startup that offers recommendations for beer based on likes and dislikes. It has been featured in the media on sites such as Lifehacker and The Huffington Post.

I am both a purveyor and supporter of open-source software. Recommendable is the recommendation engine that powers goodbre.ws, and I have since open sourced the Rails application for goodbre.ws itself. Inflections is a Ruby gem to provide more sane English singularization and pluralization rules for Ruby on Rails (dropping the number of rules from 65 to 13) as well as providing a centralized hub for internationalized inflection rules. I also contribute to existing libraries whenever I can, and have provided contributions to many libraries including Ruby on Rails and bundler.

In my free time, I trade one set of keys for another and mess around with recording songs I like. I question everything and almost always [need citations]. I have a severe caffeine addiction for which I self-medicate with a heavy prescription of espresso. Usually, I’ll add 6 oz. of milk. I am allergic to water.

You can find me as @davidcelis on Twitter, GitHub, and most other places of renown.

I am not currently looking for full-time employment, but if you’d like to collaborate on a project, check out my résumé and shoot me an email.
